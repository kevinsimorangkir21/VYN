The 6th International Technology-Enhanced Language Learning Symposium (iTELLS 2025) celebrated groundbreaking advancements in language learning, particularly in the integration of AI technologies. Held annually, this symposium has become a crucial event for educators, researchers, and innovators to explore the future of language education in the digital age. This year's theme, "Next-Gen Language Learning: The AI Revolution in the Classroom," highlighted how artificial intelligence is revolutionizing the ways we learn and teach languages.

### Gold Award Recognition: "Snake Rush"

One of the most exciting outcomes of the 6th iTELLS symposium was the recognition of outstanding projects, with the *Gold Award* going to the team behind  *"Snake Rush"* . This innovative project was presented by a talented group of students and researchers, including:

* **Anastasia Citra Negara**
* **Muhammad Raffi Firdaus**
* **John Heartman Simangunsong**
* **Josevein Hutagalung**
* **Ghendis Ayu**
* **Tirtha Radanty**
* **Kevin Simorangkir**
* **Nabila Balqis**

Their project,  *Snake Rush* , showcased the potential of using interactive gaming and AI to enhance language learning experiences. Through creative design, the team developed an engaging and educational game that supports the development of language skills in a dynamic and fun environment. By integrating AI features into the game, they provided personalized learning experiences, ensuring that each participant could progress at their own pace while staying motivated.

### The Role of AI in Language Learning

The focus on AI in language learning is one of the key features of iTELLS 2025. With the ever-growing presence of technology in education, AI has proven to be a transformative tool in creating more effective, adaptive, and personalized learning experiences. Projects like *Snake Rush* are examples of how AI can be used to craft engaging educational tools that adapt to the learner's unique needs, improving both engagement and retention.

AI-driven language learning platforms are changing the way we approach not only the acquisition of new languages but also the teaching methodologies that educators utilize. Through intelligent feedback systems, real-time error correction, and contextual learning environments, AI is providing opportunities to break down traditional barriers in language education.

### Conclusion

The 6th International Technology-Enhanced Language Learning Symposium (iTELLS 2025) stands as a beacon of innovation in the field of language education. The recognition of projects like *Snake Rush* serves as a testament to the exciting potential of AI in revolutionizing the classroom experience. As we look to the future of language learning, events like iTELLS will continue to play a pivotal role in shaping the landscape of education in the digital era.

Congratulations to the *Snake Rush* team for their well-deserved Gold Award, and to all participants who are pushing the boundaries of what is possible in the world of language learning!
